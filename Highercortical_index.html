Higher cortical processing

(an excuse to talk about neuroanatomy again)















The brain cortex has primary areas for simple processing of a specific modality (for example, primary somatosensory cortex, primary visual cortex, primary motor cortex), unimodal association cortex for more complex processing of a single modality (for example, visual association cortex, motor association cortex, auditory association cortex), and heteromodal association cortex for integration of abstract motor and sensory information, together with emotional and motivational influences provided by the limbic system.

Now it is a good time to emphasize a conceptual simplification of the visual pathways which may help. From the initial processing of visual information in the primary visual cortex, it can be conceptualized that the progressively more complex processing of that information follows two paths: the ventral pathway towards the temporal lobe in which the visual information is processed to know WHAT it is and the dorsal pathway towards the parietal lobe in which the visual information is processed to know WHERE it is.


This simplification of reality may help you remember that the area affected in Balint syndrome is in the parietal lobe (WHERE pathway) while the area affected in prosopagnosia is in the temporal lobe (WHAT pathway). This rule of thumb helps localize other syndromes.
