SUMMARY

Narcolepsy can happen with and without cataplexy and this is what defines narcolepsy type I and narcolepsy type II respectively. In narcolepsy type I the hypocretin is low. In narcolepsy type II the hypocretin is normal.

Cataplexy can be masked by SSRIs taken for other reasons.

Patients with narcolepsy can partially control when they fall asleep and it is not unusual to not fall asleep when in an stimulating environment.

The diagnosis of narcolepsy relies on a combination of clinical features and sleep studies (or LP).
