The underutilization

In the USA there are approximately 330 million people.

In the USA there are approximately 3 million adults and 0.5 million pediatric patients with epilepsy.

In the USA there are approximately 1 million people with refractory epilepsy. Among those, approximately 100,000 to 500,000 patients with refractory epilepsy are surgical candidates, but only 2,000 epilepsy surgeries are done per year.

In the rest of the world things are not better and probably are worse. In a Canadian study fewer than 2% of patients who may have benefited from epilepsy surgery received it.







How is this possible?

There are widespread misconceptions around epilepsy surgery among patients, families, and physicians (even among neurologists and pediatric neurologists). The Dunning-Kruger effect may explain part of this situation, but not the whole situation.
